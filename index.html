Usage guide

How to prepare and submit a conversion request.

How this works

This portal collects your request details and formats them into a structured message for Claude. When you click "Copy formatted request", that text goes to your clipboard. You then open a new conversation in the SDS Conversion Claude.ai Project, attach your three files, paste the request, and send. Claude — with the project's conversion instructions loaded — runs the full conversion, populates sections 8 and 15 from your data files, and returns a QC report.

The three files you need

  • Authored SDS — PDF or DOCX export from the authoring software
  • Target jurisdiction template — from your template library; the section 15 inventory framework is already pre-populated in the template
  • Section 8 & 15 data — XLSX or CSV export from your data provider; multi-substance exports are fine

CAS numbers

Always include CAS numbers in the request form, one per line. They must match the format in your data export exactly (e.g. 67-63-0 not 676300). This is how Claude finds the right rows in your data file.

What Claude does — and doesn't — do

  • ✓ Converts the SDS into the target jurisdiction template
  • ✓ Populates section 8 OELs and BEIs from your data files
  • ✓ Populates section 15 listing status using the template's inventory framework
  • ✓ QCs classification language, GHS elements, units, and terminology
  • ✓ Flags data gaps and items needing manual completion
  • ✗ Does not invent regulatory data — gaps become [DATA GAP] placeholders
  • ✗ Does not translate into other languages — flags if required
  • ✗ Does not fill in supplier info, logo, or revision date — always manual steps

Each request gets its own conversation

Always start a new conversation in the project for each conversion request. This keeps each SDS in its own clean thread, making it easier to find, review, and track. Name the conversation with the product name and target jurisdiction for easy reference.
